Drammens Tidende covers Hotvet legesenter: "Very good capacity"

28 May 2026 3 min read News

On 27 May 2026, the local Drammen newspaper Drammens Tidende ran a feature on the opening of Hotvet legesenter — Drammen's newest GP practice. The article gives a thorough overview of what we offer, who will be working here, and why this is good news for residents of a municipality that has struggled with GP capacity.

Highlights from the article

Journalist Constance Maria Enger Svanholm visited us at Rosenkrantzgata 75 and spoke with managing director Goran Dosic about the plans for the new centre. Here are the key points from the article, for anyone wondering whether Hotvet legesenter is right for them:

  • Six new GP positions open in the second week of June 2026.
  • All patient lists are "zero lists" — meaning they start without any existing patients, with 500 slots each and the option to expand over time.
  • The centre has been built with capacity for twelve GP offices in total, so there is room to grow in the coming years.
  • At the end of 2025, around 5,600 people were on the waiting list for a GP in Drammen municipality.
  • The premises were renovated by Vestviken Entreprenør Byggservice AS with sound insulation, technical installations and new entrances.
  • Total projected investment for 2026 is around NOK 3.5 million, including a setup grant from the municipality.

Capacity is the key word

Like many other municipalities in Norway, Drammen has struggled with GP capacity for several years. Hotvet legesenter is one of two new clinics the municipality has supported — the other being Drammen Helsepark — and together they add ten new GP positions in Drammen.

"Patients shouldn't have to wait long for appointments. They'll get very quick access to our GPs, and the GPs are ready to work."

Goran Dosic, managing director, to Drammens Tidende on 27 May 2026 (translated from Norwegian)

In practice, this means it is easy to register now — and that there will be capacity to see you within a reasonable time once you have switched. That is a significant difference from many established clinics, where appointment wait times can be long even for registered patients.

The GPs joining Hotvet legesenter

The article introduces the medical team:

  • Specialists in general practice: Ahmed Akram, Inva Malaj and Mari Sørum Melaaen.
  • General practitioners (ALIS — specialists in training): Anja Egilsdóttir, Frida Børsting and Hubert Shema.

In addition, several medical secretaries are already in place. You can read more about each of the GPs and secretaries on our team page.

Built for young doctors and future growth

An important point in the DT article is the motivation behind the project: Dosic explains that he wants to make it easier for young doctors to start out as GPs, by building a centre where doctors can focus on patient care and offload much of the administrative work.

"I see how very difficult it is for young doctors to get started. That's why I wanted to build a centre where the doctors can focus on patient work and avoid much of the administrative burden."

Goran Dosic, managing director, to Drammens Tidende on 27 May 2026 (translated from Norwegian)

The article also notes that the wider Hotvet area is undergoing significant development: the gym chain Feel24 will move into the ground floor of the building, an ophthalmology practice is planned for the business park, and around 120–130 new homes are being planned along the river in the Hotvetalléen project.

Local in Drammen

Hotvet legesenter is located at Rosenkrantzgata 75 — with parking right next to the entrance, which faces Rosenkrantzgata. Centrally located on Hotvet, in an area that is rapidly developing.

How to register as a patient

Want to secure one of the slots now that the lists are fresh? Here is how:

  1. Go to helsenorge.no and log in.
  2. Choose "Change your regular GP".
  3. Search for one of the doctors starting at Hotvet legesenter (see the names above) and select the GP you want.
  4. Confirm the change. Your new GP becomes active from the first day of the following month.

You can change GP up to two times per calendar year — there is no fee. Need help? See our more detailed step-by-step guide to changing your GP in Drammen.
